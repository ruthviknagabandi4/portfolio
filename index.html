Artifact 03 — Workshop 1 AI Lab

Ethics in AI Reflection Guide — A Socratic Chatbot

A working AI chatbot built on Chatbase.co that guides users through structured ethical reflection on AI scenarios using a Socratic questioning approach — designed for students, professionals, and teams grappling with AI ethics in practice.

Objective

To gain direct hands-on experience designing, building, and iterating on a functional AI assistant, applying a Design Thinking framework to produce a purpose-built tool that serves a genuine user need.

Description

The Ethics in AI Reflection Guide is a chatbot that does not give answers — it asks questions. Using a six-step Socratic framework, it guides users through identifying stakeholders, surfacing ethical frameworks, applying one framework to a scenario, encountering a complication, and forming their own reasoned conclusion.

Process

Applied Design Thinking: Empathy — identified users who want to engage with AI ethics but lack a structured thinking partner. Define — the problem is that most AI ethics resources tell people what to think rather than helping them think better. Ideate — chose Socratic approach over prescriptive bot. Prototype — built on Chatbase.co with custom system prompt. Test — verified bot asked questions rather than lecturing across multiple scenario types. Iterated three times to balance depth with usability.

Unique Value

A bot that prescribes ethical positions would be philosophically self-undermining. The Socratic design choice reflects a genuine understanding of how ethical reasoning develops — through questioning, not instruction. This makes it a more durable and trustworthy tool than a standard information bot.

Tools & Technologies

Chatbase.co Google Gemini Consensus GPT STORM AI Design Thinking Prompt Engineering

Artifact Value Proposition

As AI systems increasingly make or inform consequential decisions in healthcare, finance, and operations, the ability to reason clearly about AI ethics is a professional competency — not just an academic one. This chatbot provides a structured, accessible tool for teams and individuals to develop that competency. For organizations deploying AI, it demonstrates that I can design AI tools with genuine consideration for the humans who use them, not just the systems that run them.

Relevance

In batch operations, every automated process has a human downstream. The same is true for AI systems. Building a tool that helps people think more carefully about those human consequences reflects the operational mindset I bring to AI/ML — always asking who is affected and how.
